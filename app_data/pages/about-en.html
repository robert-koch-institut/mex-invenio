Vision

The Metadata Exchange (MEx) project is dedicated to improving the discoverability of datasets and research activities at the RKI, thereby promoting the reuse of data. How? By focusing on metadata: instead of providing data directly, the MEx Metadata Catalogue captures descriptive information about research data and research activities. This forms the basis for sharing data according to the FAIR principles.

MEx makes metadata discoverable, accessible, and shareable on a daily basis and makes it available for further research. The goal is to gain an overview of which research data exist, to understand their context, and to know what needs to be considered for reuse.

The project builds on the preliminary work conducted in the DFG-funded MaMoDaR project. Together with D4L data4life gGmbH, the RKI explored the vision for a FAIR metadata catalogue and developed concepts based on this work. The partnership with D4L concluded successfully with the completion of the pilot phase. The project is currently in development and preparing for internal rollout.

MEx is also one of the many measures supporting the implementation of the German Federal Government’s Open Data Strategy.

The code is published on GitHub.
