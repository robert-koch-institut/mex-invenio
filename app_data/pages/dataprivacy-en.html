Welcome to MEx! MEx stands for “Metadata Exchange” and is a metadata catalogue that provides you with metadata about datasets and research projects of the Robert Koch Institute (“RKI”, “we”). Using the MEx Metadata Catalogue, you can learn where and how data from RKI research processes are generated and/or used.

The protection of your personal data (“data”) is of particular importance to us. This privacy policy informs you about how we process your data in accordance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the Federal Data Protection Act (BDSG), and/or other applicable data protection regulations when you use the MEx Metadata Catalogue.

2. Scope of the processing of personal data

The following section lists the data that we may process about you in connection with MEx. The exact scope of data processing depends on which functions and services of the MEx Metadata Catalogue you use and for which you have given your consent.

2.1 Technical Usage Data

In the course of your general use of the MEx Metadata Catalogue, we process the following technical usage data about you:

  • Usage behavior through external system access
  • Browser information
  • Device information
  • Timestamps
  • IP address

Your IP address is replaced by the proxy server IP address. Your individual visitor IP address is not stored.

2.2 Usage Analytics Data

If you provide separate consent, we process the following usage analytics data for statistical purposes and to optimize the MEx Metadata Catalogue:

  1. Page Views
    • The number of visits to the site is recorded to determine how many users have accessed MEx overall. We also track how often the homepage and subpages are accessed to understand the level of detail at which users utilize the platform and to derive optimizations.
    • The number of returning visitors is recorded to assess the usefulness and interest of the platform for users.
  2. Time Spent on the Platform

    We record the average time you spend on the platform to understand the extent to which the MEx platform serves its intended purpose for you and other users.

  3. Search Queries and Filter Options

    We record how often and to what extent you use search and filter functions, in order to optimize these features.

  4. Number of Contact Requests

    We record how many contact requests you submit via the platform to researchers or data managers. This helps evaluate how MEx facilitates contact requests and thereby enables data reuse.

  5. Search Terms

    Your search terms are recorded anonymously and separately from other usage data to optimize the search algorithm and the metadata displayed in the platform.

3. Legal Basis

When you use the MEx Metadata Catalogue, the data listed under Section 2.1 are processed on the legal basis of Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR, as processing is necessary for the use of the MEx Metadata Catalogue, including error correction and abuse prevention.

If you additionally provide consent for the processing of usage analytics data (Section 2.2), these data are processed for analytical purposes to enable a user-friendly and efficient search for requested metadata, and to maintain, optimize, and further develop the functions and services of the MEx Metadata Catalogue. The legal basis for processing these data is your consent under § 25(1) TTDSG in conjunction with Art. 7(1) GDPR.

4. Data Retention

Technical usage data (Section 2.1) are stored in server logs for your visit to the MEx Metadata Catalogue for 7 days. IP addresses may be stored in audit logs for 180 days.

Usage analytics data (Section 2.2) are stored until you withdraw your consent, but no longer than 2 years.

5. Recipients

For the provision and use of the MEx Metadata Catalogue, RKI uses CANCOM Public GmbH as a service provider. CANCOM Public GmbH processes your data as a processor on behalf of and under the instructions of RKI. RKI has concluded a data processing agreement pursuant to Art. 28(3) GDPR to ensure compliance with data protection requirements.

6. Transfer to Third Countries

No transfer of data to countries outside the European Union (so-called third countries) takes place.
