Statement on Accessibility

This accessibility statement applies to the website currently accessible at https://mex.rki.de/ provided by the Robert Koch Institute.

The Robert Koch-Institute is working to make MEx accessible in accordance with the German Disability Equality Act (BGG) and the Barrier-free Information Technology (BITV 2.0). A test by the independent BITV test organisation is currently underway and the test results will then be published here. Until then, this accessibility statement is based on the internal evaluation conducted on 01 December 2025.

Status of Compatibility with Accessibility Requirements

As we regularly release new versions of our website, newly introduced non-accessible content may occasionally occur. We review this at regular intervals and strive to remedy any accessibility issues as quickly as possible. Should you nevertheless notice any content that is not accessible, we welcome your feedback at mex@rki.de.

Feedback and contact details

Arbitration procedure

The Federal Government Commissioner for Matters relating to Persons with Disabilities has an arbitration service in accordance with Section 16 BGG. The arbitration service has the task of resolving conflicts between people with disabilities and federal public bodies. You can contact the arbitration service if you are not satisfied with the answers you receive from the above-mentioned contact options. The aim is to find a solution to a problem together.

The arbitration procedure is free of charge. You also do not need legal assistance.

You can find all the information about the arbitration procedure on the service's website. There you can read how the arbitration procedure works and how to apply for arbitration. You can also submit the application in plain language or in German sign language.
